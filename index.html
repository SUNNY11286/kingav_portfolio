All published papers

Seeing in the Dark: A Different Approach to Night Vision Face Detection with Thermal IR Images

a new approach to face detection in low-light situations using thermal infrared (IR) images. The method uses a deep learning model trained on thermal IR images, which captures the thermal signatures of objects unaffected by low-light conditions. The method outperforms traditional face detection methods in low-light conditions, achieving high accuracy. The thermal signature of the face provides valuable insights for precise face detection, and the method is highly efficient in detecting faces under various pose and expression variations.

Prediction of Solar Power Generation and Maintenance Activities for 1MW Power Plant - IEEE GlobConet

Solar energy's importance in environmental sustainability is emphasized in this study. Using a semi-structured dataset, multiple models were employed to forecast solar power generation and predict maintenance activities. Tree-based models exhibited superior performance, and the challenge of predicting module cleaning in advance was acknowledged.

Computational Aspects and Statistical Modeling of Sleep Disorder - JAMSAT Journal

This paper highlights the importance of solar energy for environmental sustainability. Using a semi-structured dataset from a solar power facility, various models were employed to forecast solar power generation. Tree-based models outperformed Naive Bayesian and univariate LSTM, and challenges in predicting maintenance activities, particularly module cleaning, were acknowledged.
